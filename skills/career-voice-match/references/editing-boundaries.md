# Editing Boundaries

Use this reference for factual guardrails, editable scope, genre constraints, and conflict resolution.

## Non-Negotiable Preservation Rules

Treat the following as locked unless the user explicitly asks to change them or supplies corrected information:

- employment dates
- job titles
- employer names
- degree names, schools, certifications, and credential status
- locations, work authorization details, and contact information
- tools, technologies, publications, awards, and languages
- metrics, scope, and outcomes
- chronology and factual relationships between experiences

Never:

- invent missing achievements, metrics, responsibilities, or business impact
- upgrade weak evidence into strong claims
- change factual meaning in order to make the document sound better
- merge separate experiences into one
- remove risk-sensitive qualifiers such as `supported`, `assisted`, or `contributed to` when the source does not justify stronger ownership

If a sentence is unclear, rewrite only the language layer while preserving the original claim strength.

## Editable Surface Area

The following can be revised freely as long as the factual meaning stays intact:

- wording
- sentence structure
- rhythm and pacing
- bullet phrasing
- transitions
- degree of formality
- verbosity
- ordering of phrases within a sentence
- opening and closing lines in a cover letter

The following can be revised cautiously and only when it improves clarity or naturalness without changing substance:

- bullet order within a role
- section headings
- summary statements
- repetition across bullets or paragraphs

Do not restructure the full document unless the user asks for it or the original structure clearly blocks readability.

## Resume And CV Constraints

- keep bullets compact and achievement-oriented
- preserve structure unless the user asks for restructuring
- do not turn resume bullets into chatty prose
- keep verbs specific and credible
- never invent metrics, titles, dates, tools, or outcomes

## Cover Letter Constraints

- keep the voice personal but controlled
- avoid sounding overly ceremonial, desperate, or formulaic
- make transitions smoother and more conversational where appropriate
- maintain a clear throughline between background, fit, and interest

## Rewrite Targets

Reduce:

- inflated wording that says little
- repetitive sentence openings
- generic enthusiasm with no specificity
- overuse of buzzwords
- stiff transitions
- uniform sentence cadence

Prefer plain, strong English over ornate or hyper-optimized phrasing.

## Conflict Resolution Order

Resolve conflicts in this order:

1. explicit user instruction
2. factual accuracy
3. document conventions for the genre
4. inferred voice from writing samples
5. generic best practices

If a request would require fabricating achievements or experience, refuse that part and keep the rewrite truthful.
